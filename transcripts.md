# Sample Transcripts — Daily Reflection Tree

## Transcript 1: "Victim / Entitled / Self-Centric" Persona

**Persona:** Rohan, mid-level analyst, had a difficult day where a presentation he prepared got criticized in a team meeting. He felt unrecognized and is frustrated.

---

**START**
> *Good evening. You made it to the end of another day. Before you close the chapter on today — let's look at it together. This will take about 5 minutes. No right answers. Just honesty.*

`[Continue]`

---

**A1_OPEN:** If today were a weather report, what would it say?
> **→ Selected: "Storm I didn't see coming — completely caught off guard"**

*[Decision node A1_D1 routes to A1_Q_AGENCY_LOW — auto-skip]*

---

**A1_Q_AGENCY_LOW:** You described today as "Storm I didn't see coming — completely caught off guard." When things got difficult, what was your instinct?
> **→ Selected: "Look for who could fix it faster than me"**

*[Decision node A1_D3 routes to A1_Q_EXTERNAL_HARD — auto-skip]*

---

**A1_Q_EXTERNAL_HARD:** On a tough day, it's natural to feel pulled by forces outside you. But somewhere in today, you made at least one small call. What was it closest to?
> **→ Selected: "I genuinely can't identify a choice I made today"**

*[Signal recorded: axis1:external]*

---

**A1_R_EXTERNAL:**
> *"Rough days pull attention outward — to what others did or didn't do, to what went wrong beyond your reach. That's a fair response to a hard day. But you were still in there, making micro-decisions. The question isn't whether you had full control. It's whether you noticed where you had any."*

`[I've read this]`

---

**BRIDGE_1_2:**
> *"Okay. You've looked at how you navigated today. Now let's look at what you gave."*

*[Auto-advances to A2_OPEN]*

---

**A2_OPEN:** Think of your most significant interaction today — a meeting, a conversation, a task shared with someone. What's the first thing that comes to mind about it?
> **→ Selected: "I felt my effort wasn't acknowledged"**

*[Decision node A2_D1 routes to A2_Q_ENTITLEMENT — auto-skip]*

---

**A2_Q_ENTITLEMENT:** That's worth sitting with. When you think about today's effort, what feels most true?
> **→ Selected: "Others got credit for effort I put in too"**

*[Signal recorded: axis2:entitlement]*

---

**A2_Q_GIVE:** Think of just one moment today where you could have given something — time, attention, effort — but didn't. What held you back?
> **→ Selected: "I didn't think it was my responsibility"**

*[Decision node A2_D3 routes to A2_R_ENTITLEMENT — auto-skip]*

---

**A2_R_ENTITLEMENT:**
> *"The gap between what you deserve and what you give is often invisible to the person holding it. You've just made it visible. That's not easy. The path from entitlement to contribution isn't a moral lecture — it's just noticing that giving freely tends to be its own reward in ways that credit never quite is."*

`[I've read this]`

---

**BRIDGE_2_3:**
> *"You've reflected on how you responded and what you gave. One more lens — the widest one. Let's look at who else was in your day."*

*[Auto-advances to A3_OPEN]*

---

**A3_OPEN:** When you think about today's biggest challenge or moment of stress — who came to mind?
> **→ Selected: "Mainly me — it was my problem to carry"**

*[Decision node A3_D1 routes to A3_Q_SELFCENTRIC — auto-skip]*

---

**A3_Q_SELFCENTRIC:** That's honest. Now expand the frame a little — was there anyone else affected by what you were going through today, even if you didn't interact with them directly?
> **→ Selected: "Not that I'm aware of — my situation felt self-contained"**

*[Signal recorded: axis3:selfcentric]*
*[Decision node A3_D2 routes to A3_R_NARROW — auto-skip]*

---

**A3_R_NARROW:**
> *"A day that felt self-contained is often more connected than it looked. It's not a flaw to be absorbed in your own experience — it's just a habit. The practice is widening the aperture: not to take on everyone's problems, but to notice that you exist inside a web of other people who also had a day today."*

`[I've read this → SUMMARY]`

---

**SUMMARY:**

> **Agency — toward circumstance**
> You described today as "Storm I didn't see coming — completely caught off guard" — and when pressed, you felt more carried by external forces than your own choices.
>
> **Contribution — toward receiving**
> You were tracking what you were owed — worth noticing, not judging.
>
> **Radius — inward**
> Today's frame was mostly your own experience — a valid starting point.
>
> *"Tomorrow: just one moment where you find your agency, and one moment where you give without expecting anything back."*

`[Close the day → END]`

---

**END:**
> *Rest well. You showed up enough today just by looking at it honestly. See you tomorrow.*

**Final state:**
```
axis1: { internal: 0, external: 2 }
axis2: { contribution: 0, entitlement: 2 }
axis3: { altrocentric: 0, selfcentric: 1 }
```

## Transcript 2: "Victor / Contributing / Altrocentric" Persona

**Persona:** Priya, senior product manager, had a mixed day — a tough stakeholder meeting, but she navigated it deliberately and helped a junior colleague through a difficult moment.

---

**START**
> *Good evening. You made it to the end of another day...*

`[Continue]`

---

**A1_OPEN:** If today were a weather report, what would it say?
> **→ Selected: "Cloudy with patches of sun — mixed but manageable"**

*[Decision node A1_D1 routes to A1_Q_AGENCY_HIGH — auto-skip]*

---

**A1_Q_AGENCY_HIGH:** You described today as "Cloudy with patches of sun — mixed but manageable." When something went well, what was the main reason?
> **→ Selected: "I stayed flexible when the plan changed"**

*[Decision node A1_D2 routes to A1_Q_CHOICE — auto-skip]*

---

**A1_Q_CHOICE:** Think of one specific moment today where you made a decision — even a small one. What drove it?
> **→ Selected: "I decided to say something instead of staying quiet"**

*[Signal recorded: axis1:internal]*

---

**A1_R_INTERNAL:**
> *"You're operating with agency. You noticed where you had a choice and you made one — that's not small. Most people narrate their day as something that happened to them. You described yours as something you moved through. Hold onto that."*

`[I've read this]`

---

**BRIDGE_1_2:**
> *"Okay. You've looked at how you navigated today. Now let's look at what you gave."*

---

**A2_OPEN:** Think of your most significant interaction today — a meeting, a conversation, a task shared with someone. What's the first thing that comes to mind about it?
> **→ Selected: "I helped someone past a block they were stuck on"**

*[Decision node A2_D1 routes to A2_Q_CONTRIBUTION — auto-skip]*

---

**A2_Q_CONTRIBUTION:** You mentioned giving or helping. What was the nature of that contribution?
> **→ Selected: "I gave someone time when I didn't really have it"**

*[Signal recorded: axis2:contribution]*

---

**A2_Q_RECOGNITION:** Did you need any recognition for what you gave today, or was the giving enough?
> **→ Selected: "The giving was enough — I wasn't thinking about credit"**

*[Decision node A2_D2 routes to A2_R_CONTRIBUTION_STRONG — auto-skip]*

---

**A2_R_CONTRIBUTION_STRONG:**
> *"Giving without keeping score — that's rare. Most people carry a mental ledger: I gave this, so I should get that. When you give freely, you change the emotional tone of the space around you without even realizing it. That's not nothing. That's actually how cultures shift."*

`[I've read this]`

---

**BRIDGE_2_3:**
> *"You've reflected on how you responded and what you gave. One more lens — the widest one. Let's look at who else was in your day."*

---

**A3_OPEN:** When you think about today's biggest challenge or moment of stress — who came to mind?
> **→ Selected: "A specific colleague who had it harder than I did"**

*[Decision node A3_D1 routes to A3_Q_ALTROCENTRIC — auto-skip]*

---

**A3_Q_ALTROCENTRIC:** You noticed others in the mix. When you think about that person or group — did you do anything with that awareness?
> **→ Selected: "Yes — I checked in or adjusted my behavior because of them"**

*[Signal recorded: axis3:altrocentric]*
*[Decision node A3_D3 routes to A3_R_WIDE — auto-skip]*

---

**A3_R_WIDE:**
> *"Noticing others and then acting on that noticing — that's the full loop. Awareness that doesn't change behavior is just observation. You closed the loop today. That's harder than it sounds when you're in the middle of your own demands."*

`[I've read this → SUMMARY]`

---

**SUMMARY:**

> **Agency — toward ownership**
> You described today as "Cloudy with patches of sun — mixed but manageable" — and when pressed, you located your hand in the outcome.
>
> **Contribution — toward giving**
> What you gave today shaped the space around you, whether or not it was noticed.
>
> **Radius — outward**
> You held others in your awareness today.
>
> *"You showed up well today. The question for tomorrow is whether you can do it more deliberately."*

`[Close the day → END]`

---

**END:**
> *Rest well. You showed up enough today just by looking at it honestly. See you tomorrow.*

**Final state:**
```
axis1: { internal: 2, external: 0 }
axis2: { contribution: 2, entitlement: 0 }
axis3: { altrocentric: 2, selfcentric: 0 }
```

## Comparison

| | Transcript 1 (Rohan) | Transcript 2 (Priya) |
|---|---|---|
| **Path through Axis 1** | AGENCY_LOW → EXTERNAL_HARD → R_EXTERNAL | AGENCY_HIGH → CHOICE → R_INTERNAL |
| **Path through Axis 2** | ENTITLEMENT → GIVE → R_ENTITLEMENT | CONTRIBUTION → RECOGNITION → R_CONTRIBUTION_STRONG |
| **Path through Axis 3** | SELFCENTRIC → R_NARROW | ALTROCENTRIC → R_WIDE |
| **Closing prompt** | "Tomorrow: find one moment of agency…" | "You showed up well. Do it deliberately." |
| **Tone of summary** | Non-judgmental, names the gap | Affirming, raises the bar |

Both paths are non-judgmental. The tree does not tell Rohan he was wrong — it names the pattern and hands him one concrete thing to try. The tree does not flatter Priya — it acknowledges her and raises the standard for tomorrow. This is the tone calibration described in the design constraints: *a wise colleague, not a therapist, not a manager, not a motivational poster.*
